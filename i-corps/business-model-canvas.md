# Business Model Canvas

## Value Propositions

- Eliminates screen interruptions that increase clinical error risk by nearly 13%
- Gives nurses back time with patients
- Voice access to hospital tools without breaking workflow
- Offloads routine questions from charge nurses and managers
- Makes existing AI investments reach the nurse during care
- Generates analytics for leadership

## Customer Segments

- Bedside nurses on high-census med-surg and cardiac floors without resource nurses
- Charge nurses who absorb every clinical and operational question
- CCIO as beachhead buyer: efficiency, cost reduction, quality improvement, revenue impact
- CNOs accountable for safety outcomes they cannot currently track
- CIOs measuring ROI on clinical AI tools with low bedside adoption

## Channels

- Founder clinical credibility
- CCIO is the door
- Pilot target: med-surg or cardiac floor

## Customer Relationships

- High-touch onboarding integrating with hospital's existing systems per facility
- Dedicated customer success during pilot deployments
- Analytics dashboards tracking adoption, utilization, and knowledge gaps

## Key Activities

- Customer discovery across stakeholder segments
- Hardware and platform development
- HIPAA compliance and SOC2 security certification
- Must coexist with hospital phone for camera-dependent workflows
- Address privacy concerns
- Pilot deployment and clinical environment validation

## Key Resources

- Clinical founder with bedside credibility
- Technical team: AI/ML, embedded systems, hardware
- Validation data from I-Corps customer discovery

## Key Partners

- Component suppliers and contract manufacturers
- EHR vendors for FHIR API integration
- HIPAA-compliant hosting infrastructure partners
- NSF/I-Corps ecosystem
- Clinical AI vendors as complementary partners

## Revenue Streams

- Hardware as a Service: per-device pricing
- Software as a Service: per-facility licensing
- Non-dilutive grants

## Cost Structure

- Engineering team compensation
- Hosting infrastructure and AI platform costs
- Hardware prototyping, manufacturing, and BOM costs
- Hospital onboarding costs per client
- Compliance and security certifications
- Customer discovery and conference travel
