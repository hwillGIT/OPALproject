# Hypothesis Map

**Updated 3/13/26 (Post 39 Participants)**

## Customer Segments

- **H1:** Problem is universal in mechanism but severity tracks with unit structure, not unit type. Med-surg and cardiac floors without resource nurses are the beachhead. ICU proximity buffers it.
- **H2:** CCIO is the beachhead buyer. Efficiency is the decision framework, not safety. Safety events from information gaps are real (~5%) but explicitly not a top priority.
- **H3:** NI team is evaluator and configurer, not champion. They get activated after leadership approves. Do not pitch to NI first.
- **H4:** Nurse educators are training alignment but education does not audit tool usage. The gap between training and sustained use is unmeasured.

## Value Proposition

- **H5:** Nurses bypass hospital systems and go human first, consistently, all experience levels, both hospital systems. The gap is the interface, not the information.
- **H6:** Hospitals are deploying clinical AI but nurses do not know it exists at the point of care. NI team confirmed no usage data for clinical reference tools on the phone.
- **H7:** The Vocera-to-smartphone transition removed hands-free capability nurses still want back. 6/7 nurses confirmed. CNIO confirmed nothing hands-free exists in the market. Phone was the only option.
- **H8:** The problem is not hair-on-fire for leadership. It is chronic and normalized. The sales motion is AI ROI, not safety crisis.
- **H9:** Screen interruptions during care increase clinical error risk by nearly 13%. Cognitive overload from batching is a separate validated dimension of the problem alongside time.

## Channels

- **H10:** CCIO is the single door, not a dual CNO/CIO path. His project ingestion process requires estimated ROI.
- **H11:** Founder clinical credibility is the trust accelerator. Interviews opened because of bedside RN background.
- **H12:** Resource nurses go offline 12-5 to cover breaks, leaving charge nurses as sole routing layer. This is the specific deployment window.

## Customer Relationships

- **H13:** Managers have no visibility into silent tool abandonment. No current system tracks it.
- **H14:** Must coexist with phone, not replace it. CNIO will redirect to phone capabilities if positioned as replacement. Phone owns camera-dependent workflows.

## Key Activities

- **H15:** FHIR and existing APIs make integration feasible. Hospital authorization to grant third-party access is the real bottleneck, not engineering.
- **H16:** Infection control and privacy are real concerns. NI team raised cleaning concerns for headsets. Nurses need private space for protected information.

## Key Partners

- **H17:** Clinical AI vendors are complementary. LYNA drives bedside adoption of their products.

## Revenue Streams

- **H18:** Hospitals will pay for a device that increases utilization of existing AI investments and surfaces workforce intelligence they cannot currently get. Pricing must be additive to the phone, not replacement. Incremental spend.
