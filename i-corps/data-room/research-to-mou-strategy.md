# OPAL — Research-to-MOU Strategy

## Internal Research Brief Steps

1. Finish both I-Corps programs: 20+ interviews at Mount Sinai (Ruth), 20+ at USC (Kofi)
2. Combine the datasets: 40+ interviews across two major academic medical centers, two different health systems, two different cities
3. Code the data by theme across both sites: system bypass, tool abandonment, communication regression, routing burden, unit structure differences
4. Flag what held across both institutions versus what was site-specific — patterns that show up at both Mount Sinai and USC are significantly stronger than single-site findings
5. Anonymize completely: role titles only, no names, no unit numbers, hospitals referenced only as "Site A" and "Site B"
6. Write the brief: problem statement, methodology (semi-structured interviews, N=40+, two academic medical centers), findings by theme, implications for clinical technology design
7. Kofi reviews for technical and academic framing, clinical mentor reviews for accuracy
8. Keep as a shareable PDF for investor meetings, hospital conversations, accelerator applications, and SBIR narrative sections — not public

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks after both I-Corps programs end

## QI Pilot Proposal Steps

1. Two parallel paths: Ruth pursues Mount Sinai, Kofi pursues USC Keck Medicine
2. Each identifies a faculty sponsor at their institution to co-lead
3. Frame LYNA as a quality improvement intervention testing whether a voice-activated interface reduces information retrieval time, interruption burden, and communication delays
4. Submit QI determination requests at both institutions
5. Write the one-page proposal: problem backed by 40+ interviews across both sites, intervention (LYNA on one unit for 8-12 weeks), metrics (time to information, interruption frequency, communication response time, staff satisfaction pre/post)
6. Select pilot units at each site using I-Corps data
7. Get nursing leadership sign-off at each site — your I-Corps interviews with operational and buying process people are the relationship foundation for this ask
8. Run pilots, collect pre/post data
9. Write up results: two-site pilot data from two academic medical centers is a fundamentally different story than one hospital testing a startup product

**Timeline:** Start identifying faculty sponsors during I-Corps, submit proposals 4-6 weeks after, pilots 3-6 months after approval

## How They Connect

The research brief uses 40+ interviews from two institutions. That's not a startup guessing. That's primary qualitative research. Investors see rigor. Hospital leaders see someone who studied the problem before building.

The QI pilots at both sites give you two-site validation. That's what SBIR reviewers want. That's what Series A investors want. That's what the next 10 hospitals need to see before they say yes.

The I-Corps interviews are the foundation for everything that comes after.
